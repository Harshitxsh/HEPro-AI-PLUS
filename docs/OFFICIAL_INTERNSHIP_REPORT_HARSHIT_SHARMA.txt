ABSTRACT

The HEPro AI+ project addresses the critical challenge of student disengagement and attrition in higher education through the development of a Dedicated Mentoring System. Traditional student support mechanisms often suffer from low scalability and reactive intervention strategies. This project proposes a hybrid intelligence approach, combining expert-defined rule-based scoring with unsupervised machine learning to proactively identify at-risk students. The system calculates a Student Readiness Index (SRI) based on four key domain scores: Academic Performance Score (APS), Wellness Score (WWS), Productivity and Time Management Score (PTMS), and Career Readiness Score (CRS). Unsupervised K-Means clustering is utilized to uncover nuanced student behavioral personas, enriching the risk categories with context-driven insights. A sophisticated mentor matching engine then assigns specialized interventions based on a quantified hierarchy of needs and mentor capacity. Developed using a professional Python data stack (Pandas, Scikit-Learn), the system demonstrates high interpretability and scalability, providing a framework for personalized, data-driven mentoring at an institutional level.

1. INTRODUCTION

1.1 Background
The landscape of higher education is undergoing a paradigm shift, driven by the increasing volume of student data and the demand for personalized learning experiences. Within this context, student mentoring remains a cornerstone of academic success; however, manual mentoring processes are often insufficient for large-scale institutional needs. The HEPro AI+ project emerges as a response to the need for a systematized, data-centric approach to student support, leveraging computational intelligence to augment human expertise.

1.2 Motivation
The primary motivation for this project is the observation that student failure is rarely the result of a single factor. Instead, it is a complex intersection of academic performance, mental wellbeing, and time management. Current systems often focus solely on grades, neglecting the burnout and lack of career clarity that lead to disengagement. Building a system that integrates these multi-dimensional factors is essential for holistic student development and proactive attrition prevention.

1.3 Problem Definition
Institutions face significant challenges in identifying students who require immediate intervention. Conventional methods are often lag-indicators, occurring only after academic performance has declined. Furthermore, there is a lack of automated logic to match specific student "pain points" with the most appropriate mentoring resources, leading to inefficient use of academic and wellness staff. The absence of behavioral context in simple metric-based monitoring creates "blind spots" in student support programs.

1.4 Objectives
The core objectives of the HEPro AI+ project include:
- Designing a behaviorally realistic synthetic student dataset for system validation.
- Developing a multi-dimensional scoring engine to quantify student readiness (SRI).
- Implementing an unsupervised machine learning model to segment students into behavioral personas.
- Engineering a rule-based matching engine to automate mentor assignment based on specialized expertise.
- Creating a transparent, explainable decision intelligence report for institutional stakeholders.

1.5 Scope
The scope encompasses the design and implementation of the backend intelligence layer of the mentoring platform. This includes synthetic data generation, feature engineering, score calculation, ML modeling using K-Means, and the matching logic engine. The project focuses on the logic of intervention rather than the frontend user interface, although it provides reports suitable for UI integration.

1.6 Limitations
The current iteration of the system relies on synthetic data for initial validation. While the behavioral correlations are designed to be realistic, real-world deployment would require integration with actual Institutional Research (IR) data. The system assumes a pre-existing pool of specialized mentors for the matching logic to be effective and relies on regular data ingestion cycles to remain accurate.


2. LITERATURE REVIEW / CONCEPTUAL FOUNDATION

2.1 Traditional Mentoring Systems
Traditional mentoring in academic settings is typically characterized by high-touch, manual interactions. While highly effective in small groups, these systems lack the scalability required for modern universities with thousands of students. Coordination frequently occurs via decentralized channels, leading to inconsistent record-keeping and delayed responses to student crises.

2.2 Role of AI in Education
Artificial Intelligence in education (AIEd) has primarily focused on adaptive learning paths and intelligent tutoring systems. However, its application in pastoral and non-academic support is an emerging and critical field. AI offers the ability to process high-dimensional datasets to find patterns that human coordinators might miss, particularly regarding subtle behavioral trends indicative of burnout or loss of motivation.

2.3 Data-Driven Student Support
Modern support systems utilize "Early Warning Systems" (EWS). These systems leverage statistical models to predict attrition. HEPro AI+ builds upon the EWS concept by adding an "Action Layer," moving from simple prediction to prescriptive matching of students to specialized mentors based on quantified needs.

2.4 Hybrid AI Systems
The HEPro AI+ system utilizes a hybrid architecture. Rule-based systems provide the "Guardrails" and transparency required for ethical decision-making in education, while Machine Learning (Clustering) provides the "Pattern Recognition" needed to identify non-linear student personas. This combination ensures the system is both robust and explainable.


3. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

3.1 Project Architecture
The HEPro AI+ architecture is structured as a decoupled, multi-layered pipeline. This modular approach ensures that each component can be independently tuned or replaced without disrupting the entire system. The architecture is primarily divided into four sequential layers: Data, Intelligence, Decision, and Action.

3.2 Workflow
The end-to-end workflow follows a linear progression:
Data Generation -> Scoring & Transformation -> Behavioral Clustering -> Need-Based Matching -> Intervention Triggering.
This flow ensures that the system first quantifies raw data, then adds behavioral context through ML, and finally makes an informed, rule-governed decision regarding student support.

3.3 Module Breakdown
- Data Layer: Responsible for the ingestion and normalization of student and mentor records. It handles the storage of intermediate datasets to maintain an auditable trail.
- Intelligence Layer: Calculates the primary metrics (APS, WWS, PTMS, CRS) and the aggregate Student Readiness Index (SRI) using weighted logic.
- Decision Layer: Utilizes K-Means clustering to identify student personas and interprets these clusters to enrich the risk categories.
- Action Layer: The Matching Engine that implements the hierarchy of needs logic to assign available mentors to students.
- Feedback Layer: Generates the final decision outputs, including recommendations and high-risk alert logs.


4. DATASET DESIGN

The dataset philosophy for HEPro AI+ is "Simulated Realism." Rather than utilizing random noise, the dataset is engineered to reflect known psychological and academic correlations.

- Students Dataset: Consists of records containing GPA, attendance, assignments completion, stress levels, sleep hours, mental wellbeing indices, productivity scores, and career clarity indicators.
- Mentors Dataset: Defines available resources with attributes like mentor ID, expertise area (Academic, Wellness, Career, Productivity), and experience level (Junior, Mid, Senior).
- Interaction Logic: The system maintains a dynamic "Current Load" for mentors to ensure balanced distribution.
- Preprocessing: Includes standard scaling and normalization of scores to a 100-point scale for comparability.
- Quality Control: Implementation of range constraints (e.g., GPA <= 10, Attendance <= 100) and uniqueness checks on IDs.


5. FEATURE ENGINEERING

Feature engineering is the process of transforming raw student data into meaningful indicators for the scoring and ML models.

- Feature Selection: Key metrics like GPA, stress_level, and career_clarity were selected for their high predictive power.
- Normalization: Raw metrics (e.g., stress on 1-10) are normalized to positive performance indicators (e.g., Wellness on 0-100).
- Encoding: Expertise areas and risk categories are encoded for computational efficiency in the matching and clustering layers.
- Derived Features: 
    - SRI (Student Readiness Index): An aggregate score representing overall student health.
    - APS/WWS/PTMS/CRS: Specialized domain scores calculated from multiple raw features to provide targeted insights.


6. SCORING SYSTEM DESIGN

6.1 Rule-Based Intelligence
The scoring system utilizes expert-defined weights to ensure interpretability. Each score is designed to be independent but contributory to the overall SRI.

6.2 APS (Academic Performance Score)
APS = (GPA * 10 * 0.4) + (Attendance * 0.3) + (Assignments * 0.3). This represents the objective academic progress of the student.

6.3 WWS (Wellness Score)
WWS = ((11 - Stress) * 10 * 0.4) + (Wellbeing * 10 * 0.4) + (Sleep * 10 * 0.2). This captures the mental and physical health foundation.

6.4 PTMS (Productivity Score)
PTMS = (Productivity * 10 * 0.6) + ((11 - Distractions) * 10 * 0.4). This measures the efficiency of the student's work habits.

6.5 CRS (Career Readiness Score)
CRS = (Clarity * 10 * 0.4) + (Skill Readiness * 10 * 0.4) + (Engagement * 0.2). This gauges the student's alignment with future goals.

6.6 SRI (Student Readiness Index)
SRI = (APS * 0.4) + (WWS * 0.25) + (PTMS * 0.2) + (CRS * 0.15). The SRI is the primary filter for risk detection.

6.7 Risk Classification Logic
Students are categorized into four color-coded zones:
- Green (SRI >= 80): Thriving, requires low-touch growth opportunities.
- Blue (SRI >= 60): Stable, requires routine check-ins.
- Yellow (SRI >= 40): At Risk, requires targeted intervention.
- Red (SRI < 40): High Risk, requires immediate specialized support.


7. MACHINE LEARNING DESIGN

7.1 Role of ML
While rules define the SRI, ML provides the contextual depth. It allows the system to identify students who share behavioral traits that may not be obvious from their aggregate scores alone.

7.2 Student Segmentation
- Model Type: K-Means Clustering (Unsupervised).
- Feature Space: 11-dimensional vector including all normalized student features.
- Training Process: Optimized using the Elbow Method and Silhouette Analysis to determine K=4.
- Cluster Interpretation: 
    - Cluster 0: High-Achieving but Stressed (Hidden Risk).
    - Cluster 1: Career-Confused Underperformers.
    - Cluster 2: Stable and Balanced Students.
    - Cluster 3: High-Risk Disengaged Students.

7.3 Similarity-Based Mentor Matching
- Feature Vector: Need profiles are created based on the distance between a student's domain scores and institutional thresholds.
- Similarity Metric: Euclidean distance to mentor expertise profiles.
- Matching Logic: Filters mentors by expertise and capacity, then selects the best match using seniority-weighted load balancing.

7.4 Model Evaluation Strategy
Performance is evaluated based on Within-Cluster Sum of Squares (WCSS) and visual inspection using PCA 2D projections. The interpretability of the clusters for human mentors is a primary metric of success.


8. INTERVENTION & DECISION SYSTEM

8.1 Philosophy
The HEPro AI+ philosophy is "Need-First Intervention." The system prioritizes human health over academic metrics to ensure long-term sustainability.

8.2 Rule Engine
A prioritization hierarchy governs the matching engine: Wellness > Career > Academic > Productivity. If wellness indicators are critical, the system bypasses other needs to trigger a mental health intervention.

8.3 Intervention Levels
- Routine: Monthly mentor check-ins.
- Targeted: Bi-weekly specialized coaching.
- Immediate: 24h response required for high-risk flags.

8.4 Decision Flow
Identify Vulnerability -> Map to Expertise Area -> Assign Mentor -> Log Alert -> Report Recommendation.


9. MENTOR PLAYBOOK FRAMEWORK

The system provides mentors with "Actionable Context":
- For Academic Risks: Focus on study plans and time-blocking.
- For Wellness Risks: Focus on stress management and counseling referrals.
- For Career Risks: Focus on goal alignment and industry exposure.
- For Productivity Risks: Focus on environment optimization and discipline.
